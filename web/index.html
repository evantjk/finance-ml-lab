How this works — and what it isn't

The signal

A gradient-boosted model ranks every stock cross-sectionally by ten momentum, trend, and volatility features. Within each sector it longs the top-ranked name and shorts the bottom — so each pair is sector-neutral by construction.

Conviction-weighted

We tested it: the model's accuracy roughly doubles on its highest-conviction calls and is noise on the rest. So pairs are ranked by conviction and the weak ones are dimmed — you see the trustworthy subset first.

The risk lens

Each leg shows a 21-day volatility forecast (HAR + GJR-GARCH) and a calibrated probability of an up-day. A wide spread on two very volatile names is not the same as a good trade.

Honest limits

The model's information coefficient is ~0.03 — real but modest. Backtests use today's index members (survivorship bias, optimistic). A research demo, not financial advice.
